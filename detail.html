Best phone to buy under 35000 in nepal | Latest june update (2022)

Last Updated: 01 june 2022

When it comes to purchasing a smartphone, price is frequently a deciding factor. Furthermore, the mid-range market in Nepal, or the 35,000 price range, is extremely crowded, making it difficult to find a smartphone that meets your needs. Similarly, there are a number of smartphones that could have made our list, including the Galaxy A32, Oppo F19, and Vivo Y51, but they are all tear-jerkers in some way. So, after testing all of the phones currently available in Nepal for less than Rs. 35,000, we have chosen two smartphones that provide the best value and are worthy of our recommendation .

2. Poco X3 Pro – Best Performance under रू 35K

The Poco X3 Pro is self-explanatory. This is the smartphone for you if you want a smartphone that performs well and never leaves your hand in the middle of a game. After all, it is Nepal's only smartphone with a Snapdragon 860 chipset for less than NPR 35000. This SoC is a reiteration of the top-tier Snapdragon 855+ SoC from 2019, and it is capable of powering any smartphone game.

Moving on, the inclusion of a 120 Hz high refresh rate screen and a large battery makes it an easy winner. However, there are some blunders as well. The phone has the same form factor as the Poco X3 from last year, and it is also relatively bulky, which may not give you a sense of premium-ness. Despite the fact that the smartphone's 48MP quad-camera system produces good images, it cannot compete with the Redmi Note 10 Pro, which is available at a similar price.

POCO X3 Pro Specifications:

  • Display: 6.67-inches 120Hz FHD+ IPS LCD, 396 PPI, Gorilla Glass 6
  • Chipset: Qualcomm Snapdragon 860 (7nm Mobile Platform)
  • Memory: 6/8GB RAM, 128/256GB (expandable)
  • Software & UI:Android 11 with MIUI 12 for POCO
  • Rear Camera:Quad (48MP main, 8MP ultrawide, 2MP depth, 2MP macro)
  • Front Camera:20MP sensor (punch-hole)
  • Fingerprint sensor: Physical (side-mounted)
  • Connectivity:Hybrid Dual-SIM (Nano), WiFi 802.11 a/b/g/n/ac, Bluetooth 5.0, GPS / A-GPS / Glonass / Galileo / BDS, USB-C
  • Sensors:Accelerometer, Proximity, Gyro, Compass
  • Battery:5160mAh battery with 33W Charging
  • Price in Nepal: Rs. 31,999 (6/128GB) | Rs. 36,999 (8/256GB)

1. Redmi Note 10 Pro – Best All-rounder under रू 35K

The Redmi Note 10 Pro is at the top of our list. If you're looking for a good all-around mid-range smartphone, this is it. Despite its low price, the device has a premium feel to it. The Redmi Note 10 Pro retains all of the features that other handset manufacturers have dropped, such as the headphone jack, infrared port, and stereo speakers.

The phone is powered by a Snapdragon 732G chipset, which provides a satisfactory gaming and day to day experience. And a 120Hz AMOLED screen, long battery life, will further add to its premium experience. Though these are some of the smartphone’s highlights, the phone’s UI optimization could be improved. Similarly, the phone has a 108MP camera that can capture decent images, but with such a large sensor, Xiaomi should have surpassed the competition.

POCO X3 Pro Specifications:

  • Display: 6.67-inches 120Hz FHD+ AMOLED, 396 PPI, Gorilla Glass 5
  • Chipset: Qualcomm Snapdragon 732G (8nm Mobile Platform)
  • Memory: 6/8GB RAM, 64/128GB storage (expandable)
  • Software & UI: Android 11 with MIUI 12
  • Rear Camera:Quad (108MP main, 8MP ultrawide, 2MP depth, 5MP super macro)
  • Front Camera:16MP sensor (punch-hole)
  • Fingerprint sensor: Physical (side-mounted)
  • Connectivity:Dual-SIM (Nano), WiFi 802.11 a/b/g/n/ac, Bluetooth 5.1, GPS / A-GPS / Glonass / Beidou, USB-C
  • Sensors:Accelerometer, Proximity, Gyro, Compass, Ambient Light
  • Battery:5020mAh battery with 33W Charging
  • Price in Nepal: Rs. 33,999 (6/128GB), Rs 36,999(8/128)
